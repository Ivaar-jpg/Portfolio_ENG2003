Passion Project — Trophy Design & Fabrication

For my passion project, I designed and produced trophies for Frosh Week and the York Engineering Competition. In the past, a senior student had created acrylic trophies, but after their graduation, no documentation remained. Wanting to revive and improve this tradition, I took it upon myself to reverse-engineer the process and create something new.

I began by sketching ideas, inspired by the space-themed Frosh Week, and settled on a helmet design. I then created small-scale prototypes using 3D printing to test my ideas before moving to laser cutting, a skill I had to learn from scratch. After experimenting with materials and designs, I produced final versions using black and gold acrylic, ensuring that every member of the winning teams would receive a trophy rather than just one per team.

This project pushed me to be resourceful, creative, and persistent. Without existing documentation, I had to rely on experimentation and problem-solving to succeed. It also reflects my growth as a communicator, as I had to share my vision with peers and secure support for the project. More importantly, it shows my commitment to leaving a meaningful contribution to my community. I consider this one of my best works because it combined creativity, technical skills, and leadership, while also making a positive impact on my peers.

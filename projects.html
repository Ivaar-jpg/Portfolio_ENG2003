Trophy Design & Fabrication

I revived and improved a tradition: trophies for Frosh Week and the York Engineering Competition. With no documentation left from a past effort, I reverse-engineered the process: sketches inspired by a space-themed concept, 3D-printed prototypes, then laser-cut black/gold acrylic finals so every member of the winning teams received one.

It demanded resourcefulness, experimentation, and clear communication to align peers and secure support—combining creativity, technical skill, and leadership with tangible community impact.

Process

  • Concept sketches and theme exploration
  • 3D-printed prototypes for sizing/fit
  • Laser-cut acrylic parts (black/gold)
  • Batch assembly and quality checks

Tools & Skills

  • CAD basics, 3D printing, laser cutting
  • Fixture design, tolerances, adhesives
  • Budgeting, stakeholder comms, scheduling
